Understanding CTOS and CCRIS: Key Credit Report Differences

If you have ever applied for a mortgage or personal financing in Malaysia, you have likely heard the terms CCRIS and CTOS. Many borrowers confuse these two databases, assuming they are the same thing or that both are credit blacklist records managed by the government.

In reality, CCRIS and CTOS are distinct systems operated by different organizations. Knowing the differences is key to managing your credit status effectively.

What is CCRIS?

The Central Credit Reference Information System (CCRIS) is managed directly by Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM). It compiles credit information from participating financial institutions in Malaysia (commercial banks, Islamic banks, insurance companies, and some development financial institutions).

A CCRIS report lists your active loans, outstanding balances, credit card limits, and payment histories for the past 12 months. It does not blacklist you; it simply provides raw credit data. Lenders use this data to calculate your DSR and check for payment regularity.

What is CTOS?

CTOS is a private credit reporting agency registered under the Credit Reporting Agencies Act 2010. While they compile data similar to CCRIS, they also pull information from public sources, including legal summonses, litigation reports, bankruptcy declarations, and utility bills (like Astro, TNB, or telecom operators).

If you fail to settle an outstanding balance with a telecom provider or have a pending dispute with a trading supplier, it will show up on your CTOS report under trade references. This is data that BNM's CCRIS does not collect.

Credit Agency Check: Banks in Malaysia pull both CCRIS and CTOS reports to get a 360-degree view of your debt limits, payment histories, and legal standing before approving financing.

Key Differences Table

  • Source: CCRIS is government-managed (Bank Negara Malaysia); CTOS is a private commercial credit reporting agency.
  • Scope of Data: CCRIS tracks banking loans and commitments only; CTOS tracks banking commitments PLUS public records, legal cases, and non-bank collections (utilities).
  • Report Duration: CCRIS holds a strict rolling 12-month window; CTOS reports can retain archive records of historical settled defaults or court cases for a longer duration.

Summary
