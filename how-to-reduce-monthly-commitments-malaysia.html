How to Reduce Your Monthly Commitments and Boost Cash Flow

If you find that a large portion of your monthly paycheck is immediately channeled into settling bank commitments, you are not alone. Rising OPR rates and living costs in Malaysia mean that debt servicing can easily eat up your discretionary income, leaving little room for savings or investment.

Reducing your monthly commitments is the most effective way to restore positive cash flow. Here are three practical, bank-approved strategies to achieve this.

1. Consolidate Unsecured Liabilities

Unsecured debts—like credit cards and personal loans—carry the highest interest rates. Juggling multiple credit cards with outstanding balances at 15% to 18% per annum is incredibly expensive.

By consolidating these balances into a single Term Personal Financing facility at a lower interest rate, you can simplify payments and reduce your total monthly commitment significantly.

2. Property Refinancing (Cash Out)

If you own residential or commercial property in Malaysia that has appreciated in value over the years, you can refinance your mortgage. By locking in a new mortgage at a lower interest rate (around 4% to 4.5%) and utilizing a cash-out facility, you can clear off your high-interest personal loans and credit cards.

Because mortgage repayment tenures can extend up to 35 years, this strategy drastically lowers your immediate monthly cash outflow, although it increases the overall interest paid over the life of the mortgage.

Caution: Refinancing carries valuation, legal, and processing fees. Ensure the monthly cash flow savings outweigh these upfront entry costs before proceeding.

3. Request Restructuring Programs

If you are facing temporary cash flow disruptions, do not wait for payments to lapse. Approach your primary banks to discuss restructuring terms. Extending loan tenures, converting revolving credits to fixed terms, or requesting a short-term moratorium are standard banking practices to help borrowers recover.
