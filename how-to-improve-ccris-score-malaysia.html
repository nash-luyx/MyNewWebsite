How to Improve Your CCRIS Score: A Practical Guide for Malaysians

Your credit score is one of the most critical factors that Malaysian banks look at when deciding whether to approve your home loan, car loan, or business financing. At the heart of credit evaluations is the CCRIS report, managed by Bank Negara Malaysia.

If your CCRIS report contains missed payments, late fees, or high credit card utilization rates, it can lead to immediate rejections. Fortunately, CCRIS is a rolling 12-month record, meaning you can improve your score with consistency.

Understand What CCRIS Records

The CCRIS report updates monthly and shows the payment status of all your borrowing accounts over the past 12 months. Each account displays a record of "0" (paid on time), "1" (one month late), "2" (two months late), and so on. A credit officer wants to see a clean run of "0"s across all facilities.

Actionable Steps to Clean Up Your CCRIS

1. Clear All Arrears Immediately

If you have outstanding payments marked as "1" or "2", prioritize clearing them immediately. Banks will not approve new applications until all active arrears are brought to current status (restored to "0").

2. Pay on Time, Every Time

Set up automatic standing instructions or calendar alerts to ensure payments are cleared before the due date. Consistent on-time payments over a few months will gradually push older late-payment markers out of the rolling 12-month report window.

3. Keep Credit Card Utilization Below 70%

Consistently maxing out your credit cards flags you as a high-risk borrower. Try to keep your credit card balances below 70% (ideally 30%) of the total approved limit. If necessary, request a credit consolidation program to pay down cards.

Pro-Tip: If you have no credit history at all, banks may reject you due to lack of track record. Consider taking one basic credit card, using it for small payments, and clearing the balance in full every month to build a positive score.

Conclusion

Improving your CCRIS record requires patience, but the rewards include lower interest rates and faster bank approvals.
